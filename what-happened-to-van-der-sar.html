What Happened to Van Der Sar? Is Van Der Sar Retired?

Edwin van der Sar, a retired professional football player, is currently receiving medical supervision due to a brain hemorrhage.

He retired in 2011 at the age of 52.

Who Is Van Der Sar?

Edwin van der Sar, a former Dutch professional football player, is widely regarded as one of the greatest goalkeepers in the history of the sport.

He rose to prominence during the early 1990s as part of Ajax’s renowned golden generation.

Van der Sar played a vital role in Ajax’s triumph in the UEFA Champions League in 1995.

In 1999, he transferred to Juventus before making his way to England.

Van der Sar joined Fulham before eventually signing with Manchester United in 2005.

His time at Manchester United was filled with remarkable achievements, including winning the Champions League for the second time in 2008.

He became one of only eight players to have won the prestigious competition with multiple clubs.

Although Van der Sar retired from professional football in 2011, he made a brief comeback in 2016 to participate in a match for the Dutch amateur team VV Noordwijk, where he had previously played during his youth.

Over the course of his illustrious career, Van der Sar accumulated an impressive collection of 26 major titles, primarily achieved with Ajax and Manchester United.

Notably, during the 2008-2009 season, he set a remarkable world record by going 1,311 minutes without conceding a goal in the league.

At the age of 40 years and 205 days, Van der Sar became the oldest player to win the Premier League in 2011.

His exceptional skills and contributions to the game earned him numerous individual accolades, including the titles of Best European Goalkeeper in 1995 and 2009, as well as UEFA Club Goalkeeper of the Year in 2009.

Despite the seriousness of the situation, his agent maintains an optimistic outlook regarding his recovery.

Following his swift transfer to the intensive care unit on Friday, Van der Sar underwent treatment for a brain bleed.

Ajax, his former club where he served as chief executive, has confirmed his stable condition and expressed their support.

Is Van Der Sar Retired?

In 2011, Edwin van der Sar, then 52 years old, announced his retirement from professional football.

Throughout his remarkable career, he proudly represented the Netherlands in 130 international matches.

Van der Sar’s journey in the sport included remarkable achievements at the club level.

He triumphed in the prestigious UEFA Champions League, winning the title with both Ajax and Manchester United.

His success wasn’t limited to Europe, as he secured four Premier League championships during his tenure with Manchester United.

The talented goalkeeper also made notable contributions to other renowned clubs like Fulham and Juventus.

Van der Sar’s exceptional skills between the posts played a pivotal role in the triumphs of his teams, earning him great admiration and respect as one of the most accomplished players in his position.

His ability to perform at the highest level and make crucial saves during important matches solidified his esteemed status in the world of football.

Following a distinguished career spanning many years, Van der Sar ultimately chose to retire, leaving behind an indelible legacy.

His contributions to both his clubs and his country, exemplified by his Champions League triumphs and domestic league successes, firmly establish him as one of the revered figures in the annals of football history.

In a retirement declaration, Van Der Sar revealed that he had been contemplating the end of his career ever since his wife, Annemarie, experienced a brain hemorrhage in December 2009.

On this particular day in 2011, Edwin Van Der Sar, the esteemed goalkeeper of Manchester United, made a momentous announcement regarding his retirement, which was set to take place at the conclusion of the ongoing season.

The former Dutch international, then 40 years old, had joined Old Trafford from Fulham in 2005 and played a pivotal role in the team’s remarkable achievements, contributing to four Premier League titles and a triumphant Champions League campaign in 2008.

One of Van Der Sar’s most unforgettable moments occurred during the European Cup final against Chelsea in Moscow.

Van Der Sar Life

Edwin van der Sar, the retired professional football player, and his wife, Annemarie van Kesteren, have been blessed with two children.

Their wedding ceremony took place on May 20, 2006, at the Beurs van Berlage in Amsterdam, marking a special milestone in their lives.

A remarkable incident unfolded during the UEFA Euro 2004 tournament involving Van der Sar and his son, Joe.

His son, Joe, was on the field and jubilantly celebrated his father’s heroic act, creating a heartwarming and unforgettable father-son moment.

In December 2009, the family faced a tragic event when Annemarie van Kesteren was admitted to the hospital just two days before Christmas.

Reports indicated that she had suffered a suspected brain hemorrhage and was in a “very poor” condition, requiring immediate medical attention.

In response to his wife’s critical state, Van der Sar took an indefinite leave from his football commitments to be by her side during this challenging time.

Fortunately, there was a positive update regarding Annemarie’s health.

This undoubtedly brought relief to the family and those close to them, after enduring such a difficult and uncertain period.
